  • • Date of Examinations: Varies

    • STAGES OF EXAMINATION


    Stage 1: Prelimimary Examinations- Objective type

    Stage 2: Mains written Examinations- Objective & Descriptive type

    Stage 3: Personality test/Group Discussion- Interview type

    Preliminary Examinations (Objective Type)


  • • Date of examination: Notified IBPS & SBI
  • • Number of papers: 1
  • • Duration of each paper: Composite time of 1 hour
  • • Each paper shall be of a maximum of 100 marks (100 questions- 1 marks each)
  • • Type of examination: Objective type (MCQs)
  • • There are five alternatives for the answers to every question.
  • • There will be negative marking for incorrect answers. For each wrong question one-fourth (0.25) of the marks assigned to that question will be deducted as penalty.
  • • Language of examination: English and Hindi
  • • Cut off marks: Varies.

    • MAINS Examinations (Objective & Descriptive Type)

  • Date of examination: As per the notification
  • • Number of papers: Two
  • • Duration of each paper: Paper 1: 3 hours & Paper 2: 30 minutes
  • • Paper 1 will be for 200 marks & Paper 2 will be for 25 marks
  • • Type of examination: Objective & Descriptive type
  • • Language of examination: English and Hindi
  • • How many will clear Mains? 10 times the vacancies notified for recruitment will be selected to write Mains examination
  • • Language of examination: English and Hindi
  • • Cut off marks: Varies

    Syllabus for Mains


    Quantitative Aptitude:

    Number Systems, Ratio & Proportion, Percentage & Averages, Profit & Loss, Mixtures &Alligations, Simple Interest & Compound Interest, Surds & Indices, Time & Distance, Mensuration – Cylinder, Cone, Sphere, Sequence & Series, Permutation Combination & Probability, Quadratic Equations, Data Interpretation


    Reasoning Ability:

    Sitting Arrangements, Tabulation, Logical Reasoning, Syllogism, Input Output, Coding Decoding, Alphanumeric Series, Ranking / Direction / Alphabet Test, Data Sufficiency, Coded Inequalities, Non Verbal Reasoning.


    English Language:

    Reading Comprehension, Cloze Test, Error Spotting, Sentence Correction, Para Jumbles, Vocabulary, Multiple Meaning Words, Paragraph Completion and New Pattern Questions of various types


    Computers:

    Number System, History of computers, Hardware, Software, Database (Introduction), Communication (Basic Introduction), Networking (Lan, Wan), Internet (Concept, History, Working Environment, Application), Security Tools, Virus, Hacker, MS Windows & MS Office, Logic Gates.


    Current Affairs:

    Banks in NEWS, Economy based current affairs, Business NEWS, Agreements, New Appointments, Visits, Government Schemes, Awards and Honors, Summits, Committees, National and International, Obituaries, Reports and Indexes, Books and Authors, Defense, Sports.


    Banking:

    RBI, Function of RBI, Banking Abbreviations, Banking Regulation Act 1949, Policy Rates, Types of Accounts, NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENTS ACT 1881, BANKING OMBUDSMAN SCHEME 2006, FINANCIAL INCLUSION, PRIORITY SECTOR LENDING, MONEY MARKET INSTRUMENTS, Capital Market.



    Personality test

  • • Marks:100 Marks
  • • This will be added to written marks
  • • Selection based on = Written Marks + Personality test
  • • Interview will be conducted by a board ( 1 chairman and 4 expert members)
  • • Questions will be based on general topics, banking and questions based on candidate’s graduation stream.
  • • Objective of the interview: To assess the personal suitability of the candidate for a career in Banking Sector.
  • • What is expected from the candidate:
  • 1. Mental alertness.
  • 2. Banking knowledge.
  • 3. General Awareness.
  • 4. Balance of judgement.
  • 5. Variety and depth of interest.
  • 6. Knowledge of financial system.
  • • How many will be called for interview: 3 times the vacancies
  • • Duration of Interview: 10-20 minutes
